Revamp Proposal — Retail-to-IB EDM Journey

8 sections · New structure, messaging, CTAs, and strategy
Click to expand
1
New Journey Philosophy
Current Journey
Educates → Educates → Educates → Confuses → Repeats
Proposed Journey
Hooks with money → Proves it works → Removes objections → Creates urgency → Closes hard
The shift is from "here's what an IB is" (brochure mode) to "here's how much you could earn" (sales mode). Every email should answer the reader's real question: "What's in it for me, and why should I do this now?"
2
Proposed New Structure — 5 Emails, 7 Days, Zero Gaps
EmailDayAnglePsychology
E1Day 0The Money HookCuriosity — lead with earnings, not education
E2Day 2Social ProofTrust — show it works for real people
E3Day 4Objection HandlingConfidence — "it's easier than you think"
E4Day 6UrgencyScarcity — reason to act NOW
E5Day 7Final CloseDecision — short, direct, last chance
Why this spacing: D0, D2, D4, D6, D7. Every-other-day cadence keeps momentum without feeling spammy. The final two emails (D6 → D7) land back-to-back for a closing one-two punch.
3
Email-by-Email Breakdown
E1 — Day 0: The Money Hook
Subject line idea: "What would an extra $500/month look like?"
Core ContentOpen with a specific earning scenario: "Refer 5 friends who each trade 20 lots/month → you earn $800–$1,100/month in commissions." State the numbers clearly: $8–$11 per lot, paid daily. Quick 3-step visual: Sign up → Share your link → Earn daily.
CTASee How Much You Could Earn
What's different from current D0: Leads with money, not education. The reader immediately knows the financial upside before learning what an IB even is.
E2 — Day 2: Social Proof
Subject line idea: "They started just like you — now they earn daily"
Core Content2–3 earning scenarios or anonymised IB profiles (e.g. "A trader in SEA referred 8 friends, now earns $600+/month passively"). Highlight TMGM's genuine differentiators: daily payouts (not weekly like XM), PAMM/MAM support, Sub-IB structure, dedicated account manager. Reinforce: "You're already trading with us — why not earn from your network too?"
CTAStart Your IB Application
What's different: Replaces the generic "5 perks" (old D1) with proof that real people earn real money. Shows TMGM-specific advantages, not generic broker claims.
E3 — Day 4: Objection Handling
Subject line idea: "Think it's complicated? Here's what it actually takes"
Core ContentAddress the top 3 objections: (1) "I don't have time" → It's passive income. Your referrals trade, you earn automatically. (2) "I don't know enough people" → You only need 3–5 active traders to start seeing meaningful income. (3) "I don't know how to promote" → TMGM gives you: IB Portal dashboard, ready-made banners and creative materials, personal tracking link, dedicated account manager.
CTAApply in 2 Minutes
What's different: Addresses real hesitations instead of assuming the reader has already decided. The resources (IB Portal, Creative Materials, Support) are presented as answers to doubts, not features of a program.
E4 — Day 6: Urgency
Subject line idea: "Your enhanced rate expires in 5 days" (Option A) or "%%First_Name%%, your spot is still open" (Option B)
Option AIf business can support an onboarding incentive — limited-time enhanced commission: e.g. "$12/lot for your first 90 days" (vs standard $8–$11). Or: "First 50 new IBs this month get a dedicated onboarding call + enhanced rate." Countdown language, deadline is real.
Option BIf no incentive available — recap the strongest points from E1–E3. "You've seen the numbers. You've seen the proof. The only thing left is to start." Frame as opportunity cost: "Every day you wait is a day your network is trading without earning you commissions."
CTA"Lock In Your Rate" (Option A) or "Start Earning Today" (Option B)
What's different: Creates a reason to act NOW. Current journey has zero urgency across all 7 days.
E5 — Day 7: Final Close
Subject line idea: "%%First_Name%%, last reminder — then we'll stop asking"
Core ContentShort email. 3–4 sentences max. No more education. "We've shown you the numbers, the proof, and the tools. Now it's your call." Recap the single most compelling stat (e.g. "$8–$11 per lot, paid daily"). Acknowledge this is the last email: "This is the final email in this series. If you're interested, the door is open."
CTAApply Now
What's different from current D7: Doesn't repeat D0. It's a short, direct, respectful close — not another brochure. Acknowledging it's the last email is a pattern-break that actually increases clicks.

The Finfluencer Question
Recommendation: Remove Finfluencer from this journey entirely. The Retail-to-IB journey should stay focused on one conversion goal. Introducing a second option mid-funnel creates choice paralysis.
Two alternative approaches:
A
Separate Journey
Create a dedicated Finfluencer EDM journey targeting a different audience segment (content creators, social media traders). This gives the Finfluencer program its own space to shine without diluting the IB conversion funnel.
RecommendedSeparate Flow
B
One-Line Mention
Add a single line in E1: "More of a content creator? Ask us about our Finfluencer program →" with a link. One sentence, not a full email. Acknowledges the option without derailing the IB pitch.
AlternativeMinimal Disruption
